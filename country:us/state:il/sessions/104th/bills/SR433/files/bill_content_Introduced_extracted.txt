Full Text of SR0433 ILGA.GOV SR0433 - 104th General Assembly SR0433LRB104 15527 ECR 28692 r1SENATE RESOLUTION 2    WHEREAS, The members of the Illinois Senate are saddened 3to learn of the death of Jo Marie Bonell, who passed away on 4September 21, 2025; and 5    WHEREAS, Jo Bonell attended St. Ignatius Grade School and 6St. Scholastica High School, where she served as Student 7Council President; she graduated from the theater department 8and received her Juris Doctor from Loyola University; she 9later received her Master of Library and Information Science 10from the University of Illinois Champaign; and 11    WHEREAS, Jo Bonell practiced law in Chicago for many 12years; she most recently served as the library director of the 13Des Plaines Public Library, which brought her great joy and 14fulfillment; and 15    WHEREAS, Jo Bonell enjoyed reading, gardening, traveling, 16listening to the Beatles, and laughing with her many friends; 17and 18    WHEREAS, Jo Bonell will be remembered for her humor, her 19generosity, and her devotion to her friends, co-workers, and 20family; and   SR0433- 2 -LRB104 15527 ECR 28692 r1    WHEREAS, Jo Bonell was the daughter of John and Jeanine 2Bonell; the sister of Marianne (Jump), Margaret (the late 3Thomas) Suerth, Kathy (Aaron) Crane, Jerry (Barb) Bonell, and 4Carolyn (Time) Saylor; the aunt of 11 nieces and nephews; and 5the great-aunt of three grand-nieces and grand-nephews; 6therefore, be it 7    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 8ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that we mourn the passing of 9Jo Marie Bonell and extend our sincere condolences to her 10family, friends, and all who knew and loved her; and be it 11further 12    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 13presented to the family of Jo Bonell as an expression of our 14deepest sympathy.
