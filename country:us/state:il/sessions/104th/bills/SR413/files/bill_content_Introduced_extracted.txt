and 12    WHEREAS, Gary Pileski served as a trustee of the Village 13of Roselle from 1973 to 1977; he further served his community 14as an active member of the Roselle Lions Club, an adult leader 15in 4-H, and a coach of youth intramural sports; and 16    WHEREAS, Gary Pileski was a member of Roselle United 17Methodist Church, where he chaired the preschool board and 18served in various roles over the decades; and 19    WHEREAS, Gary Pileski was a lifelong University of 20Wisconsin-Madison football fan; and   SR0413- 2 -LRB104 15235 ECR 28385 r1    WHEREAS, Gary Pileski was a devoted husband, father, 2grandfather, and friend; he was known for his kindness, 3loyalty, and compassion for others; he will be remembered for 4his quick wit, steady presence, and quiet strength; and 5    WHEREAS, Gary Pileski is survived by his wife of 39 years, 6Alice; his children, Hobie Pileski, Missy (Martin) Stewart, 7Heidi Hames, Jody (Letty) Pileski, Cody (Oscar) Guerrero, and 8David (Kathryn) Pileski; his bonus children, Sarah (Timm) and 9Daniel; 20 grandchildren; one great-grandchild; and his 10brothers, Rob Pileski and Larry Pileski; therefore, be it 11    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 12ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that we mourn the passing of 13Gary Charles Pileski and extend our sincere condolences to his 14family, friends, and all who knew and loved him; and be it 15further 16    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 17presented to the family of Gary Pileski as an expression of our 18deepest sympathy.
